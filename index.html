June 2026 Mineral

Foraged clay

The severn estuary, which brings the second largest tide gap in the world through Weston-super-Mare, deposits an ample supply of clay beside it's banks. Timed on a warm sunny day before the last high tide of the day, the deposited clay should dry as sheets easily collected ready for dry processing. Once collected and dried, the clay is pulverised then seived to a soft powder. For resistance to thermal expansion a temp can be added between 10-25% ratio to clay; Sand, charcoal, grog, these buffer against breakage while heating.
